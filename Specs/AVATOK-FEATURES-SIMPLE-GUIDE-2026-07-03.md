# AvaTOK — What You Can Do With It (Simple Guide)

*Written for everyday users. No tech talk. Everything below is live in the app today — and during the launch period, all of it is completely free.*

---

## 1. Messaging

**What it is:** Chat with anyone on AvaTOK — one-to-one or in groups. Send text, photos, videos, voice notes, files, stickers and GIFs, polls, your location, and contact cards.

**Nice little touches:**
- Voice notes are automatically turned into text, so you can read them when you can't listen.
- Long-press any message to react with an emoji; double-tap to send a quick heart. You can see who reacted.
- Forward a message to many people or groups in one go.
- Search inside a chat to find an old message.
- Set messages to disappear automatically (after 1 hour, 1 day, or 1 week) for private conversations.
- Ticks show your message status: sent, delivered, and read (blue ticks).
- Long-press a message and tap Translate to read it in your language — only you see the translation.
- Create a quick poll (question + options) and watch votes come in live.
- Share live location — friends see you move on a map until you tap "Stop sharing."

**Use cases:**
1. Planning a family dinner? Drop a poll in the group ("Friday or Saturday?") and let everyone vote — no endless back-and-forth.
2. Meeting a friend in a busy market? Share live location so they can walk right to you.
3. Got a voice note while in a meeting? Just read the automatic transcript instead of playing it.

## 2. Ava In Your Chats (@ava and #ava)

**What it is:** Ava is the built-in AI assistant, and she lives right inside your chats. You never have to leave the conversation to get something done.

- Type **@ava** followed by your request — Ava answers **privately**. Only you see the reply.
- Type **#ava** — Ava answers **in the open**, so everyone in the group sees what she brings in.

**What Ava can do from inside a chat:**
- Answer questions on the spot (facts, ideas, drafting text).
- Send and fetch emails for you (once you've connected Gmail or Outlook — see "Connected Apps" below).
- Search your own saved notes and files and bring the answer into the chat.
- Generate an image right in the conversation ("#ava draw a birthday banner").

**Use cases:**
1. You're chatting with a customer and they need a document. Instead of switching apps, type "@ava email the price list to john@example.com" — Ava sends it while you keep chatting.
2. In a team group chat, someone asks for last week's plan. Type "#ava find the email about next week's schedule" — Ava pulls it in and **everyone in the group can see it**.
3. Stuck on wording? "@ava help me politely decline this offer" — Ava drafts it privately; you copy, tweak, and send.

*Good to know: Ava will tell you honestly if she only drafted an email rather than sent it. Pulling files straight from Google Drive and adding calendar events are built and coming soon.*

## 3. Connected Apps (AvaApps)

**What it is:** Link your everyday accounts to Ava so she can act on them for you. **Available now: Gmail and Outlook.** Google Drive, Calendar, Docs, and Sheets are on the way.

**Use cases:**
1. Connect Gmail once, then from any chat ask Ava to send, find, or reply to emails — no app-switching.
2. A small business owner can answer "did the supplier reply?" right from a chat: "@ava check my inbox for mail from Acme."

## 4. Group Chats

**What it is:** Create a group, name it, and add your contacts. Groups have the full messaging toolkit — text, media, voice notes, stickers, polls, location, contact cards, reactions.

**Nice little touches:**
- Group owners and admins manage members; owners can promote others to admin.
- No limit on how many people can chat in a group.
- Mute noisy groups anytime.
- Ask #ava anything and the whole group sees her answer — great for shared info.

**Use cases:**
1. Run a class parents' group: pin down the picnic date with a poll, share the venue location, and mute it during work hours.
2. A sales team shares leads: "#ava" pulls up the product one-pager so every member sees the same document.

## 5. Voice & Video Calls (1-to-1)

**What it is:** Free voice and video calls to any AvaTOK user. Start with voice and switch on your camera mid-call whenever you like.

**Nice little touches:**
- Mute, speaker, and camera buttons during the call.
- Your screen stays awake for the whole call.
- Missed calls show a notification with a one-tap "Call back" button.
- A full call history lives in the Calls tab.
- Callers hear a ringback tone while waiting — pick your favourite from the built-in collection so callers hear *your* tone.

**Use cases:**
1. Call grandma abroad for free — and flip the camera on so she can see the kids.
2. Missed a call while driving? One tap on "Call back" from the notification when you park.

## 6. Group Audio & Video Meetings

**What it is:** Group chats have a call button too. Tap it and up to **25 people** can join a group meeting — audio or video — right from the chat.

**Nice little touches:**
- The person speaking is highlighted automatically, so you always know who's talking.
- Everyone controls their own mute, camera, and speaker; there's a participants list.
- The person who started the call can end it for everyone.

**Use cases:**
1. A weekly team stand-up: no meeting links, no invites — just tap the call icon in the team group.
2. Extended-family catch-up: 15 relatives across 3 countries in one free audio room.
3. Study group before exams: hop into the group call, and drop notes into the chat while you talk.

## 7. Dialpad & Your AvaTOK Number

**What it is:** Every user can claim a **free AvaTOK number**. It works like a phone number inside AvaTOK — and here's the best part: **anyone can dial it from their AvaTOK dialpad without saving you as a contact first.** Just type the number and call.

**Nice little touches:**
- Pick your own number — even search for a pattern you like (e.g. one containing "777").
- Share it easily: a QR code, a printable card, or a link (avatok.ai/add) that opens the app directly.
- Your most-dialed people appear at the top of the dialpad for one-tap calling.
- When an unknown number calls you, you see the number and can save it as a contact in one tap.
- You control who can add you: everyone, only people who know your number, or nobody.

**Use cases:**
1. A shop prints its AvaTOK number on the storefront and flyers. Customers dial it straight from the dialpad — no "add contact" step, no app-store hunting (the share link installs the app if needed).
2. Meet someone at an event? They scan your QR code and can call you seconds later.
3. Want a memorable number for your side business? Search for one with your lucky digits.

## 8. AI Receptionist — Ava Answers Your Calls

**What it is:** Turn on the receptionist and Ava becomes your personal phone assistant. If you don't pick up within **6 rings**, Ava answers for you: she greets the caller, answers their questions, and takes a message.

**Nice little touches:**
- Ava speaks the caller's language — 30+ languages to choose from.
- Pick a greeting style from 12 presets, or write your own (there's even a festival-greeting mode).
- Leave Ava an "availability note" ("I'm in a meeting until 3pm") and she'll tell callers — with an expiry time so it doesn't go stale.
- Upload a simple info sheet (opening hours, prices, directions) and Ava answers customer questions from it.
- After each call you get **both** the written transcript and the voice recording, delivered like a normal chat message.
- Works on unknown callers too — you see their number and can save them as a contact.

**Use cases:**
1. A salon advertises its AvaTOK number. When the owner is mid-haircut, Ava picks up, tells the caller today's opening hours and prices, and takes the booking request — the owner reads the transcript between clients.
2. On a flight? Set the availability note ("Travelling, back Monday") — every caller hears it, and every message is waiting in your chat when you land.
3. Screen unknown callers: let Ava answer first, read what they wanted, and decide whether to call back.

## 9. Safety — Ava Guardian

**What it is:** Built-in protection that watches out for you, quietly.

- **Message requests:** When a stranger messages you first, the chat is held in a safe area — their photos stay blurred and you choose Accept, Block, or Report before any real conversation starts.
- **Scam check:** In any stranger chat, tap the shield icon and Ava checks the conversation for scam patterns — you get a clear "Likely scam" warning or an all-clear. Warnings are private; the other person never knows.
- **Smart replies:** In 1-to-1 chats, three tap-to-send suggested replies appear so you can answer fast.
- **Family safety:** If a child's account is linked to a parent, the parent gets safety alerts and a weekly digest — without reading the child's messages.

**Use cases:**
1. A "bank official" messages your mum out of the blue. The message lands in requests, photos blurred; one tap on the shield and Ava flags it as a likely scam — Block, done.
2. A parent gets a weekly safety summary about their teen's account — peace of mind without spying.

## 10. Ava Companion — Your Own AI Chat

**What it is:** A private space to talk with Ava directly. Ask anything, brainstorm, practise a new language with the language-tutor mode, or use "Discuss with Ava" to paste in a tricky conversation and get advice on how to reply (Ava never sends anything for you — you stay in control).

**Use cases:**
1. Practise English or Spanish daily with the tutor persona — free, judgement-free.
2. Not sure how to reply to a difficult message from your landlord? Discuss it with Ava and get a calm, well-worded draft.

## 11. Storage & Backup (AvaLibrary / AvaStorage)

**What it is:** Your personal cloud drive inside AvaTOK — **5 GB free**. Store any file, organize into folders, and open your photos in a built-in viewer.

**Nice little touches:**
- Copying a file into a second folder never uses extra space — it's counted once.
- Even if you ever go over your quota, your files are **never deleted**.
- **Back up & restore:** Connect Google Drive and AvaTOK backs up your chats and media, fully encrypted. Get a new phone, sign in, and everything comes back — no recovery codes to remember.

**Use cases:**
1. Phone stolen? Buy a new one, log in, restore from Drive — chats, photos, groups all back.
2. Keep receipts, IDs, and warranties in one folder you can pull up from anywhere.

## 12. The Marketplace — Where Your AI Agent Does the Talking

**What it is:** A marketplace we're rolling out where you can list anything — a car, a property for sale or rent, electronics, furniture, services, jobs — and where the real magic is **agent-to-agent conversations**. Every user has a personal AI agent. You choose its name, its voice, and its language in Settings (25 languages, including 9 Indian regional languages, and a range of voices), plus how it behaves: friendly, professional, or brief.

**How it works:** You see a listing you like. Instead of messaging the seller and waiting, you tap **"Talk to the seller's agent."** You tell your agent your maximum price and your must-haves — and then the two AI agents talk it out. A short while later, a **voice recording of the whole conversation lands in your messages**: you literally listen to your agent and the seller's agent negotiating the price or asking your questions, with a written transcript in your own language right below it.

**Nice little touches:**
- Sellers set protection rules: a price floor their agent will never go below, and an "ask me before committing" option so no deal closes without their nod.
- Quiet hours: negotiations wait politely if the seller has set do-not-disturb times.
- The two agents can bridge languages — the seller can speak Hindi and you Tamil; you still get the conversation and transcript in your language.
- A green bubble in your inbox means a deal was reached; a yellow one means no deal — either way you hear exactly why.
- A quick one-time selfie check is required before publishing a listing, keeping bots and fake sellers out.

**Use cases:**
1. **Buying a car:** You spot a car listed at 8 lakh. Tap "Talk to my agent," set your max at 7.2 lakh and "must have: full service history." Later, you play the recording: your agent pressed on price, the seller's agent confirmed the service records, and they settled at 7.4 lakh — your call whether to take it.
2. **Buying an apartment:** Your agent asks the seller's agent all the boring-but-vital questions — parking, maintenance charges, water supply, society rules — and the full Q&A sits in your inbox to replay anytime.
3. **Renting a flat:** Before you even visit, your agent checks deposit, pet policy, furnished or not, and earliest move-in date. Ten listings, ten recordings, zero phone calls made by you.
4. **School or college admissions:** An institution lists its information page; parents' agents ask about admission criteria, fees, class sizes, transport, and facilities. Every family gets the answers in their own inbox — in their own language — and the school's staff never repeats itself.
5. **Hiring a service:** Need a wedding photographer or a plumber? Your agent asks three listed providers about availability, packages, and price — you compare the recordings over your morning tea and pick one.
6. **Selling anything, effortlessly:** List your old sofa before bed with a price floor of 80%. Overnight, your agent handles five interested buyers' agents within your rules. You wake up to one accepted deal and no haggling stress.
7. **Cross-language deals:** A buyer from another state whose language you don't speak? No problem — the agents handle the conversation, and each side reads and hears it in their own language.

*Status: in development — rolling out soon. The agent settings, negotiation engine, and inbox delivery are built; we're switching the marketplace on for everyone shortly.*
